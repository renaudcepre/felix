Le Banquet de Thornwall
Moyen-Age fantastique
1247-06-21
Grande Salle de Thornwall

The great hall of Thornwall is packed. Lord Varn presides over the banquet from his stone throne. To his right, Borin, the castle blacksmith. To his left, Elara, his spy. All three sit at the same table, sharing the same meal.

Borin is a fifty-two-year-old man, stocky, with calloused hands and a face weathered by the flames of the forge. His grey hair is cropped short.

Elara sits across from him. She says nothing, scanning the hall with her gaze.

VARN
Tomorrow, I'm sending you both to the col de Brume. Borin, you know the paths. Elara, you know the shadows.

BORIN
I'm too old to run through the mountains.

VARN
At fifty-two, you still carry anvils. You'll survive.

Elara nods. She hasn't touched her plate. Borin taught her blade work when she was twelve. Since then, he has been her mentor.

ELARA
We leave before dawn.

The banquet continues. Varn raises his glass. Borin and Elara are the last to leave the hall.
