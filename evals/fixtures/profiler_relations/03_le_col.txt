Le Col de Brume
Moyen-Age fantastique
1247-06-23
Col de Brume

The fog is thick at the Col de Brume. Borin, Elara, and Darya advance together — the herbalist rejoined them at dawn, claiming to know a shortcut.

Darya is thirty years old, with tanned skin and green eyes. She bears a scar on her chin, a souvenir from an ambush in the marshes. She is quick-witted and talks nonstop.

DARYA
My father was a soldier. He always used to say: in the fog, trust your ears.

Three enemy scouts spring from the rocks. Elara fires an arrow and hits the first one. Borin charges with his axe. Darya drops flat to the ground and doesn't move.

BORIN
Behind the rock! Cover me!

Elara looses two quick arrows. The scouts fall back. Darya gets up, trembling. She pulls bandages from her bag and tends to a scrape on Borin's arm.

DARYA
You two fight well.

ELARA
We had a good teacher.

She glances at Borin. He shrugs.

BORIN
Forty years of smithing in these mountains. You learn to fight or you die.

The trio continues on its way. Darya stays at the rear, gathering plants along the edge of the path. Borin guides the group through a narrow passage. They emerge on the other side of the pass.
