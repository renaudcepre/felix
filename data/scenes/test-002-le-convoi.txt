2061: Le Convoi (Marco Ruiz)

The ore hauler rolled across the cracked plains of the former Lake Titicaca. Marco Ruiz had been driving for eighteen hours, eyes bloodshot, one hand on the wheel and the other on the pulse rifle resting on the passenger seat.

— Three kilometers, he breathed, checking the GPS. Three kilometers and we're at the depot.

Beside him, Lena Voss — in transit to Neo-Santiago after her rotation on Helios-3 — watched the road with a nervousness that betrayed her inexperience on solid ground.

— There's someone behind us, she said, pointing at the rearview mirror. For twenty minutes now.

Marco glanced up. Two headlights, steady, at a fixed distance. Raiders or a corporate escort — either way, nothing good.

— Hold on, he said, slamming the accelerator.
